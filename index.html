Abstract

In the realm of visual tasks, Masked Image Modeling (MIM) combined with Vector Quantization (VQ) has witnessed remarkable achievements in self-supervised pretraining and image generation. However, a significant hurdle persists: most existing methods grapple with simultaneously addressing two critical technical challenges. To surmount these obstacles, we introduce MergeVQ, a groundbreaking unified framework that seamlessly integrates token merging and VQ techniques.

Introduction

Vector Quantization (VQ) has emerged as a widely adopted technique in visual tasks. MergeVQ leverages this synergy by decoupling and recovering semantics during the encoding and reconstruction processes.
